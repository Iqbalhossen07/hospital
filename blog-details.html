5 Simple Lifestyle Changes for a Healthier Heart

By Dr. Evelyn Reed
| August 19, 2025

Maintaining a healthy heart doesn't require a complete life overhaul. By incorporating a few simple, evidence-based habits into your daily routine, you can significantly reduce your risk of cardiovascular disease and improve your overall well-being. Here are five key lifestyle changes recommended by our top cardiologists.

1. Embrace a Heart-Healthy Diet

What you eat has a direct impact on your heart. Focus on a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Try to limit your intake of processed foods, sugary drinks, and saturated fats. The Mediterranean diet is an excellent example of a heart-healthy eating plan.

"The human body is designed to move. Consistent physical activity is one of the most powerful tools we have for preventing chronic disease."

2. Get Moving Regularly

Regular exercise strengthens your heart muscle and helps maintain a healthy weight. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise, like brisk walking or cycling, each week. Even small changes, like taking the stairs instead of the elevator, can make a big difference.

3. Prioritize Quality Sleep

Sleep is not a luxury; it's a necessity for heart health. During sleep, your body repairs itself, and your blood pressure and heart rate decrease. Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night. If you suffer from sleep apnea, seek treatment, as it can significantly increase your risk of heart problems.
